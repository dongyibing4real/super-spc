## Design Context

### Users
Process engineers, yield engineers, and manufacturing quality leads inside semiconductor fabs. They are trained statisticians who use JMP, Minitab, and internal tools daily. They open this tool during escalations, OOC events, and shift reviews — often under time pressure, sometimes in cleanroom environments with gloves. They expect statistical rigor, information density, and immediate trust.

### Brand Personality
Precise. Authoritative. Uncompromising.

### Emotional Goal
**Controlled tension** — dense-comfortable (tighter than SaaS, looser than Bloomberg), audit-ready, rewards expertise and speed. The interface should feel slightly intense, like a cockpit instrument panel. Not calming, not alarming — focused. Every element earns attention through information value, not decoration. Audit-readiness means traceability: every chart result traceable to its data, limits, transforms, and methodology.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Command-Center Analytical — Palantir Blueprint density meets JMP interaction precision
- **Theme:** Dark-first (`--bg-0` #111418 base). Chart island uses warm light surface (#F6F7F9), not pure white
- **Typography:** Inter (UI) + IBM Plex Mono (data). Dense scale: body at 12px, labels at 10px, eyebrows at 9px
- **Spacing:** 4px base unit, dense-comfortable. Panel padding 8-12px, not 16-24px
- **Border radius:** Max 8px. Prefer 4px or 0px for analytical surfaces
- **References:** Palantir Blueprint, JMP Control Chart Builder, Bloomberg Terminal
- **Anti-references:** Notion, Linear, Vercel, Stripe — too airy, too marketing-adjacent, too much whitespace

### Accessibility
Enhanced for fab environments:
- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum (4.5:1 contrast ratios)
- Keyboard navigation throughout
- Screen reader labels on all interactive elements
- Consideration for bright ambient light (cleanroom viewing)
- Consideration for gloved interaction (44px minimum touch targets on interactive elements)
- Reduced motion support for vestibular sensitivity
- Colorblind-safe chart encoding (shape + color, never color alone)

### Design Principles
1. **Information density over whitespace** — Every pixel earns its place through data or affordance. Dense is correct for this domain; airy is wrong.
2. **Trust through precision** — Monospace numbers, explicit units, visible methodology. Engineers trust tools that show their work.
3. **Zero decoration** — No gradients, no illustrations, no marketing energy. Borders and spacing create hierarchy, not ornament.
4. **Progressive disclosure** — Show the essential at rest, reveal depth on interaction. The recipe rail collapses; layers hide until expanded; chip editors open inline.
5. **Controlled tension** — The interface should feel like a cockpit, not a lounge. Slightly intense, reward expertise, surface urgency clearly.
